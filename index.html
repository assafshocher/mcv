Modern Computer Vision

Computer vision today is driven by deep networks, and the way we design, train, and reason about them also relies on core ideas from image formation, geometry, and signal processing. To do research and build genuinely new methods, you need both: learning-based tools and a real understanding of images themselves. MCV is roughly 70% deep learning for vision and 30% the broader mathematical and algorithmic foundations that remain essential in modern vision. The course operates across the full spectrum, from fundamental mathematical ideas to state-of-the-art methods and practice. The goal is to get to the bottom of things, build real intuition, and encourage creative new thinking. It has a research orientation and concludes with a final research project.
